impact

The way we treat the dead is environmentally unsustainable. Everyday more than 6,500 people die in the United States alone. We need a solution to body disposal that reflects the values of the new generations.

A single cremation uses the same amount of energy as it takes to power an average American home for 6 days.

The traditional process of interment puts 30 million board feet of hardwoods, 100,000 tons of heavy metal, and enough emblaming fluid to fill8 Olympic sized pools each year.

Green burials require access to private land, costly transportation, and is still widely inaccessible in urban regions of the United States.

APPROACH

Throughout our lives, we consume energy from our environment. Our process gifts our nutrient-rich bodies back to the living after we die. Our process uses mycelia spores and dermestid beetles to aid in the natural decomposition process, reducing the body to bone relics, which is then recycled for calcium or returned to the family of the deceased.
