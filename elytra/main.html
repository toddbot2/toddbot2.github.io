impact

Everyday, more than 6,500 people die in the United States. There is an ever-growing need for land allocated to those who have passed on. With the growing popularity of green-burials, that need is being passed on to the forests. The trees can not keep up with the need. Burning the deceased at the rate of those who pass, has exposed the living to harmful toxins and has darkened our skies. We need a new solution.

A single cremation uses the same amount of energy as it takes to power an average American home for 6 days.

The traditional process of interment puts 30 million board feet of hardwoods, 100,000 tons of heavy metal, and enough emblaming fluid to fill 8 Olympic sized pools each year.

Green burials require access to private land, costly transportation, and is still widely inaccessible in urban regions of the United States.

 I want my last act on earth to help nourish the future. 

- Oliver N.

APPROACH

Our process gifts our nutrient-rich bodies back to the living after we die. Our process uses mycelia spores and dermestid beetles to aid in the natural decomposition process, reducing the body to bone relics, which is then recycled for calcium or returned to the family of the deceased.

The Elytra Process

Arrival

Upon arrival, the body is received and gently washed.

Preperation

Fungal spores are introduced to aid in the reintegration process.

Dermestidae

Biomass is transfered to natural absorbers further aiding the reintegration process.

Decomposition

Remains are reduced to their base elements and cleaned in preperation for final processing.

Powerification

Remains are reduced to a simple inert powder.

Return

Relics are returned to the family at the end of the process. They may also be donated for complete reintegration in the environment.
